2. **The Platinum Card from American Express** — Amex Platinum offers the strongest lounge access package in the market, including Centurion Lounge access plus a broad set of partner lounges, while also charging no foreign transaction fees. The annual fee is high, but for travelers who actually use the lounge network and credits, it can be unmatched.
3. **Capital One Venture X Rewards Credit Card** — Venture X is arguably the best value premium travel card right now, offering a compelling welcome bonus, Priority Pass and Capital One Lounge access, and no foreign transaction fees at a lower effective cost than many rivals. It is especially appealing for travelers wanting premium perks without Amex Platinum’s coupon-heavy structure.
4. **Citi / AAdvantage Executive World Elite Mastercard** — This card is a great choice for travelers loyal to American Airlines because Admirals Club access can be more useful than generic lounge memberships if you fly AA often. The sign-up bonus can be very attractive, though the card is less flexible for non-AA travelers.
5. **United Club Infinite Card** — For United flyers, this card combines no foreign transaction fees with full United Club membership and a respectable bonus. It is best for travelers who consistently fly United and value airline-specific lounge access over transferable points.
6. **Marriott Bonvoy Brilliant American Express Card** — This card is worth considering for frequent hotel travelers because it offers premium travel benefits, no foreign transaction fees, and airport lounge access via Priority Pass enrollment. It is less compelling as a pure airfare card, but strong for travelers already invested in Marriott.
