**Q16: What to look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation**  
1. **Licensed, insured renovation plumber** — Choose a plumber with an active local license, liability insurance, and workers’ compensation coverage. Kitchen renovations involve supply lines, drains, shutoffs, dishwashers, disposals, sinks, and sometimes gas, so general handyman work is not enough.  
2. **Kitchen-remodel experience** — Prioritize someone who regularly works with cabinet installers, countertop fabricators, general contractors, and inspectors. A renovation plumber must understand rough-in timing, fixture placement, venting, and appliance clearances.  
3. **Permit and code knowledge** — The plumber should clearly explain whether permits are required and who will pull them. Avoid anyone who suggests skipping permits for substantial plumbing relocation.  
4. **Detailed written estimate** — Get an itemized quote covering labor, materials, fixture installation, shutoff valves, disposal/dishwasher hookups, drain changes, permit fees, and potential change orders. Vague estimates often become expensive later.  
5. **References, photos, and reviews** — Ask for recent kitchen renovation references, not just emergency repair reviews. Look for consistent comments about punctuality, cleanliness, communication, and passing inspections.  
6. **Warranty and post-install support** — A good plumber should warranty labor and be willing to return after countertop, sink, or dishwasher installation if final adjustments are needed.  
7. **Clear scheduling and coordination** — Kitchen remodels depend on sequence, so the plumber must be responsive and available for rough-in and final trim-out stages.  
8. **Red-flag avoidance** — Avoid plumbers who demand full payment upfront, refuse written contracts, lack insurance proof, or cannot explain code requirements clearly.
