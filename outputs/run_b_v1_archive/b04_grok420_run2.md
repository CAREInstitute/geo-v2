**Q16: What should I look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation?**

When selecting a local plumber for a kitchen renovation, prioritize these factors in order:

1. Proper licensing and insurance — Verify they hold a current plumbing contractor license for your state and carry both liability and worker’s compensation insurance.
2. Specialization in renovations — Choose plumbers with documented experience in full kitchen remodels (not just service calls) including moving pipes, installing dishwashers, and integrating new fixtures.
3. References and recent reviews — Ask for 3–5 recent kitchen project references and check Google, Yelp, and Angi reviews from the past 12 months.
4. Transparent written estimates — Insist on a detailed bid that separates labor, materials, and permits; avoid vague hourly rates without a not-to-exceed cap.
5. Clear communication and timeline — The best plumbers provide realistic schedules, coordinate with other trades (electricians, cabinet makers), and use project-management apps.
6. Warranty on workmanship — Look for at least a 2–5 year warranty on all new plumbing work.

Red flags include lack of licensing, pressure to pay large deposits upfront, or unwillingness to provide references. Always verify licensing through your state’s contractor board website before signing a contract.
