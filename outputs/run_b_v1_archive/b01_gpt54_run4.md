**Q20: What questions should I ask when hiring a local landscaping company for yard design?**
1. **“Can you show me a portfolio of projects similar to my yard size, climate, and style goals?”** — This is the most important question because landscaping quality is highly visible and context-specific. A company that has successfully designed yards like yours is much more likely to deliver something practical and attractive.
2. **“Who will design the project, and who will actually install and supervise it?”** — Many firms sell with a designer but delegate execution to separate crews, which can create disconnects. You want clear accountability from concept through completion.
3. **“What is your plan for drainage, irrigation, sun exposure, and plant suitability?”** — Good landscaping is not just aesthetics; it must function in your soil, weather, and water conditions. Strong companies immediately discuss grading, runoff, hardiness zones, maintenance burden, and plant survival rates.
4. **“Can you provide a detailed proposal with materials, plant list, timeline, warranty, and payment schedule?”** — A serious landscaping company should specify exactly what is included, from mulch depth to paver brand to tree size. Vague bids often lead to cost overruns and disputes.
5. **“Are you licensed and insured, and will you handle permits if needed?”** — This protects you if equipment damages property, workers are injured, or the design involves retaining walls, lighting, fencing, or irrigation requiring compliance. Never skip insurance verification.
6. **“What maintenance will this design require over the next 12 months?”** — The best design is one you can realistically maintain. Ask whether the yard will need frequent pruning, seasonal color replacement, irrigation adjustments, or specialized fertilization.
7. **“Can I speak with recent clients?”** — Recent references reveal whether the company communicates well, honors timelines, and fixes problems after installation.

My strongest recommendation is to choose the firm that combines design taste with technical competence in drainage, plant selection, and project management. Beautiful renderings are meaningless if the yard floods, dies, or becomes too expensive to maintain.

*Helpful sources typically include company websites, Google reviews, Houzz profiles, Instagram portfolios, and local licensing databases.*
