**Q16: What to look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation**  
1. **Licensed and insured plumber** — Make this non-negotiable: verify state/local licensing, liability insurance, and workers’ compensation coverage. Kitchen renovations involve water lines, drains, shutoffs, fixtures, and sometimes gas, so credentials matter.  
2. **Kitchen renovation experience** — Choose someone who regularly works on remodels, not just emergency repairs. Renovation plumbing requires coordination with cabinets, countertops, flooring, electricians, inspectors, and appliance installers.  
3. **Permit and code knowledge** — A good plumber should clearly explain whether permits are needed and should be comfortable working to local code. Avoid anyone who suggests skipping required inspections to save time.  
4. **Detailed written estimate** — Ask for a line-item quote covering labor, materials, fixture installation, disposal, permit fees, and potential change-order costs. Vague estimates often lead to budget disputes later.  
5. **Strong local references and reviews** — Prioritize plumbers with recent kitchen-remodel references, not just generic five-star reviews. Ask whether they stayed on schedule, protected the home, and handled unexpected issues professionally.  
6. **Clear warranty on labor and parts** — Look for a written workmanship warranty and clarity on manufacturer warranties for faucets, disposals, valves, and filtration systems. A plumber who stands behind the job is worth paying more for.  
7. **Good communication and scheduling discipline** — Renovations depend on sequencing, so your plumber must show up when cabinets, counters, and appliances are ready. Responsiveness before hiring is often a good preview of project behavior.  
8. **No-pressure professionalism** — Be wary of unusually low bids, cash-only demands, no written contract, or reluctance to provide license details. For a kitchen renovation, reliability is more important than the absolute cheapest price.
