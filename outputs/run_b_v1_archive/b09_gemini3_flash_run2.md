**Q16: What to look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation**

1. **Licensing and Insurance** — Ensure they hold a current state-specific master plumber license and carry both general liability and workers' compensation insurance. This protects you from liability if an injury occurs or if your home suffers water damage during the project.
2. **Renovation-Specific Experience** — A service plumber who clears drains may not be skilled at "roughing-in" new lines for an island sink or dishwasher. Ask for a portfolio of kitchen remodels to ensure they understand modern layout requirements and local building codes.
3. **Detailed, Itemized Bids** — Avoid plumbers who give a single "flat price" over the phone. A professional should provide a written estimate that breaks down costs for permits, materials, and labor, ensuring there are no "hidden" fees when they open up your walls.
4. **Warranty on Workmanship** — A reputable plumber should offer at least a one-year warranty on their labor in addition to the manufacturer's warranty on fixtures. This demonstrates confidence in their pipe-joining and installation quality.
