**Q20: What questions should I ask when hiring a local landscaping company for yard design?**

Hiring for yard design is distinct from hiring for weekly mowing; it involves construction, horticulture, and permits. The questions you ask must reveal whether the company is a legitimate design-build firm or simply a mowing crew overextending their capabilities.

1.  **"Are you licensed, bonded, and insured for hardscape construction?"** — This is the most critical question. Many landscaping companies carry basic liability for mowing but lack the insurance coverage for heavy machinery or excavation; if they damage your property or a worker is injured on your property, you could be liable without this specific coverage.
2.  **"Can you provide a scaled drawing or 3D rendering of the design?"** — You must ask for a formal design plan rather than a verbal agreement. A professional company will provide a scaled drawing that specifies plant counts, material quantities, and drainage plans, preventing "scope creep" and ensuring you know exactly what the finished product will look like before a shovel hits the ground.
3.  **"What is the warranty on plant material and hardscaping?"** — Landscaping is a living installation, and plants often die. A reputable company will offer a 1-year warranty on trees and shrubs (provided you follow their care instructions) and a multi-year warranty on hardscape features like patios and retaining walls, demonstrating confidence in their workmanship.
4.  **"Who will be the project manager on-site?"** — You need to know if the person designing the project will be the one managing the crew. Often, the salesperson is charismatic, but the crew is unsupervised; ensuring there is a dedicated foreman or project manager on-site daily ensures the design intent is followed and communication remains clear.

*Note: No web sources were consulted; recommendations are based on general contractor hiring standards.*
