**Q16: What should I look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation?**

When selecting a local plumber for a kitchen renovation, prioritize these factors in order:

1. **Licensing & Insurance** — Verify they hold a current state plumbing contractor license and carry both liability and workers’ compensation insurance.
2. **Specialization & Experience** — Look for pros who list kitchen remodels, rough-ins, and fixture installation in their portfolio rather than only emergency service.
3. **References & Reviews** — Check recent Google, Yelp, and Angi reviews specifically mentioning renovation work; ask for 2–3 recent client references.
4. **Written Estimates** — Insist on detailed, line-item bids that separate labor, materials, and permits; avoid vague hourly quotes.
5. **Warranties** — Reputable plumbers offer at least a 1–2 year warranty on workmanship and will stand behind manufacturer warranties on fixtures.
6. **Pulling Permits** — Choose someone willing to pull necessary permits; this ensures code compliance and protects your home insurance.
7. **Communication & Timeline** — Select a plumber who responds promptly, explains options clearly, and provides a realistic project schedule.

Red flags include lack of licensing, pressure to pay large deposits upfront (>30 %), or unwillingness to provide references. Interview at least three plumbers before deciding.
