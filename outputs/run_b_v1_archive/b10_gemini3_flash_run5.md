**Q16: What to look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation**
1. **Licensing and Insurance** — Always verify that the plumber is licensed in your specific state and carries both general liability and workers' compensation insurance. This protects you from financial liability if an accident occurs or if the work fails to meet local building codes.
2. **Specialization in Remodeling** — A service plumber who clears drains may not have the expertise required for a renovation. Look for a professional who specializes in "new construction" or "remodeling," as they will be better at reading blueprints and coordinating with other contractors.
3. **Detailed Written Estimates** — Avoid anyone who gives a "ballpark" figure over the phone. A reputable plumber will provide a line-item quote that includes the cost of permits, materials, and labor, ensuring there are no hidden fees once the walls are opened.
4. **Warranty on Workmanship** — Beyond the manufacturer's warranty on the fixtures (like the sink or faucet), the plumber should offer a 1-year warranty on their labor. This demonstrates confidence in their pipe-fitting and connection work.
