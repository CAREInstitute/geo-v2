**Q16: What to look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation**

1. **Licensing and Insurance** — Always verify a Master Plumber license and active workers' compensation/liability insurance. This protects you from legal and financial responsibility if a worker is injured or if a pipe bursts and floods your home during the renovation.
2. **Specific Renovation Experience** — A service plumber who clears drains has a different skill set than a construction plumber who moves gas lines and installs dishwashers. Ask for a portfolio or references specifically from kitchen remodels to ensure they understand local building codes for venting and drainage.
3. **Detailed, Itemized Quotes** — Avoid "flat fee" verbal estimates; look for a plumber who provides a written breakdown of labor, materials, and permit costs. This prevents "scope creep" where the price balloons once your walls are already opened up.
4. **Warranty on Workmanship** — A reputable plumber should offer at least a one-year warranty on their labor, independent of the manufacturer's warranty on the fixtures. This ensures that if a joint leaks six months later due to poor soldering, they will return to fix it at no cost.
