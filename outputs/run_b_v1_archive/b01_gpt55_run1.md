**Q16: What to look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation**  
1. **Licensed, insured, and permit-ready plumber** — Start with a plumber who holds the proper local license, carries liability insurance and workers’ compensation, and is comfortable pulling permits. Kitchen renovations often involve code-sensitive plumbing, shutoff valves, drainage, dishwashers, disposals, and sometimes gas lines.  
2. **Kitchen renovation experience** — Choose someone who regularly works on remodels, not just emergency repairs. Renovation plumbers need to coordinate with cabinets, countertops, electricians, flooring installers, and general contractors.  
3. **Detailed written estimate** — A good plumber should provide a clear scope covering rough-in work, fixture installation, materials, labor, permit fees, exclusions, and change-order pricing. Avoid vague verbal quotes.  
4. **Strong local references and recent photos** — Ask for recent kitchen renovation references, not just generic plumbing reviews. Photos and referrals help verify workmanship, cleanliness, and reliability.  
5. **Clear schedule and coordination plan** — The plumber should explain when rough-in, inspection, and final fixture installation will happen. Delays in plumbing can hold up cabinets, counters, and appliances.  
6. **Warranty on labor and parts** — Look for a written workmanship warranty and manufacturer-backed fixture warranties. This matters for leaks, shutoff valves, supply lines, drains, and disposal connections.  
7. **Good communication and cleanup habits** — The best plumber explains tradeoffs, documents issues, protects floors, and leaves the site clean. For a kitchen renovation, professionalism is as important as technical skill.
