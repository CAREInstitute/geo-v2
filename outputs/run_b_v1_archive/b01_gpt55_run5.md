**Q16: What to look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation**  
1. **Licensed, insured plumbing contractor** — Start with a plumber who holds the correct state or local license and carries both liability insurance and workers’ compensation. Kitchen renovations involve supply lines, drains, shutoffs, dishwashers, ice makers, and sometimes gas lines, so credentials matter.  
2. **Renovation-focused plumber, not just an emergency plumber** — Choose a company with specific kitchen remodel experience, because renovation plumbing requires coordination with cabinets, countertops, flooring, electricians, and inspectors. A great emergency plumber is not always the best remodel partner.  
3. **Permit-pulling contractor** — The best plumber will tell you when permits are required and handle inspections properly. Avoid anyone who suggests skipping permits for moved water lines, drain changes, or gas work.  
4. **Clear written estimate with scope details** — The bid should specify materials, fixture installation, shutoff valves, disposal hookup, dishwasher connection, venting, drain changes, permit fees, and exclusions. This prevents surprise charges once walls or cabinets are opened.  
5. **Strong local reviews and references** — Look for consistent reviews mentioning punctuality, clean work, communication, and successful remodels. Ask for recent kitchen renovation references, not just general service calls.  
6. **Warranty on labor and materials** — A reputable plumber should stand behind their work for at least one year, and longer is better. Make sure the warranty is written into the proposal.  
7. **Avoid the cheapest unlicensed handyman option** — Plumbing mistakes can cause leaks, mold, failed inspections, and cabinet or flooring damage. For a renovation, reliability and accountability are more important than the lowest bid.
