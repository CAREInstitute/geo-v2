**Q16: What to look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation**  
1. **Licensed, insured plumbing contractor** — This is the non-negotiable first filter. Verify the plumber’s license, liability insurance, and workers’ compensation coverage before discussing price.  
2. **Kitchen renovation experience** — Choose a plumber who regularly handles sink relocations, dishwasher lines, refrigerator water lines, garbage disposals, shutoff valves, venting, and code-compliant drain work. Renovation plumbing is more complex than basic repairs.  
3. **Permit and inspection knowledge** — A good plumber should know when permits are required and should be comfortable coordinating inspections. Avoid anyone who suggests skipping permits for work that legally requires them.  
4. **Detailed written estimate** — Ask for an itemized quote covering labor, materials, fixture installation, shutoff valves, drain changes, rough-in work, finish work, and possible change-order pricing. A vague quote often leads to budget surprises.  
5. **Strong local references and reviews** — Prioritize plumbers with recent reviews specifically mentioning remodels or kitchen projects, not just emergency repairs. Ask for photos or references from similar jobs.  
6. **Clear coordination with your general contractor or cabinet installer** — Kitchen plumbing must line up with cabinet layouts, countertop installation, appliance specs, and backsplash timing. A plumber who communicates well can prevent costly rework.  
7. **Warranty and post-installation support** — Look for a written labor warranty and clear support if leaks, low pressure, drain issues, or fixture problems appear after installation.
