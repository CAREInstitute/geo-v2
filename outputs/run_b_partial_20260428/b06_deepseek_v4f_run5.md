**Q16: What should I look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation?**

When selecting a plumber for a kitchen renovation, prioritize these specific criteria over price alone:

1.  **Licensing, Insurance, and Bonding (Mandatory)** — Confirm they hold a current state/ municipal plumbing license (e.g., Journeyman or Master), general liability insurance (at least $1 million to cover accidental damage to your house), and workers' compensation insurance. A licensed master plumber is required for new gas lines, main drain reroutes, and water heater relocation. Ask for proof of insurance, not just a verbal claim.
2.  **Specialization in Renovations vs. Repairs** — Not all plumbers handle renovations well. Renovation work requires knowledge of drainage slope calculations for new runs, rough-in heights for dishwashers/trash compactors, and coordinating with electricians (e.g., garbage disposal wiring). Ask: "How many kitchen renovations have you completed in the last 12 months?" Look for a portfolio of similar projects with before/after photos.
3.  **Detailed Written Estimate with Scope & Materials** — Avoid verbal quotes. The estimate should list: all labor items (e.g., "remove and replace sink supply lines"), specific materials (e.g., "1/2-inch PEX-A," not just "pipe"), fixture brands you are providing (e.g., Kohler faucet), and a clear timeline (start date, estimated completion, buffer days). Request a permit if the renovation involves moving gas lines or adding a new rough-in.
4.  **Warranty and Post-Installation Support** — Ask for a warranty on both parts (manufacturer's) and labor (minimum 1 year, preferably 2-5 years). A good plumber will return free of charge if a leak occurs within the warranty period. Avoid any plumber who says "parts only" warranty.
5.  **Reputation and References** — Call 2-3 recent clients (provided by the plumber) to ask: "Did they show up on time? Did they clean up thoroughly? Were there any surprise charges?" Check Google Reviews, Angi, and the local Better Business Bureau. Look for a pattern of complaints about "unfinished work" or "ignoring code violations."

*Red flags to avoid:*
- **No written contract** or "dollar limit" verbal quotes.
- **Cash-only requests** (tax evasion or insurance fraud risk).
- **Asking for 50%+ deposit** before work begins (10-20% is standard).
- **Using unlicensed helpers** (only the master plumber is licensed; helpers should be apprentices under supervision).

*Note: For a kitchen renovation, budget 10-15% of the total renovation cost for plumbing (excluding fixtures). A reputable plumber will also perform a "rough-in inspection" and a "final check" before backsplash installation.*
