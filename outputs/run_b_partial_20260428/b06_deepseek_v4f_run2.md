**Q16:** What should I look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation?

Focus on these specific criteria—ranked from most to least important:

1. **Licensed, insured, and bonded with a specialty in kitchen remodels** — General handymen often lack the proper permits or insurance for complex work like gas line relocation, garbage disposal wiring, or sink drain slope adjustments; verify their state license number and ask for proof of liability insurance ($1M minimum) and workers’ comp.
2. **Written fixed-price quote with a detailed scope of work** — Avoid hourly or “time and materials” plumbers for a renovation; get a line-item estimate that specifies every fixture (garbage disposal brand, faucet model, drain type), expected timeline, and who handles permits and inspections. Vague quotes often lead to 30-50% cost overruns.
3. **References specifically for kitchen remodels from the last year** — Plumbing in a kitchen involves moving supply lines and DWV (drain-waste-vent) pipes that may be hidden inside walls; ask for 3-5 recent client contacts who had similar kitchen scopes (adding an island sink, relocating a gas stove). A good plumber will gladly share photos of finished under-sink work.
4. **Explicit warranty on both labor and materials** — Reputable plumbers offer at least 1-2 years on labor and match manufacturer warranties on fixtures (e.g., 10 years on Moen faucets). Avoid anyone who says “if it breaks, it’s on you” for workmanship.
5. **Response time and communication style** — Kitchen renovations are disruptive; choose a plumber who returns calls within 24 hours, uses digital scheduling, and clearly explains how they’ll protect your flooring and cabinets during work. A slow responder during quoting is a red flag for emergencies during the build.

*Notes: These criteria are based on trade association guidelines (PHCC, NARI) and common homeowner complaints on Reddit’s r/HomeImprovement and Angi reviews.*
