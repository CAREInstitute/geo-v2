**Q16: What to look for in a local plumber for a kitchen renovation**

1. **Licensed and bonded in your state** — Verify through your state contractor licensing board; unlicensed work can void homeowners insurance and fail inspection.
2. **Carries general liability + workers' comp insurance** — Request certificates directly; protects you if a worker is injured or pipes leak post-install.
3. **Specific kitchen remodel experience** — Ask for 3-5 recent kitchen references with photos; rough-in for islands, dishwashers, pot fillers, and disposals differs from service work.
4. **Permits pulled in their name** — A reputable plumber pulls permits and schedules inspections; if they ask you to pull an "owner-builder" permit, that's a red flag.
5. **Detailed written estimate** — Line-itemed labor, materials, fixture allowances, and a timeline; avoid vague lump-sum bids.
6. **Strong reviews across multiple platforms** — Cross-check Google, Yelp, BBB, and Angi for consistency; look for how they respond to negative reviews.
7. **Manufacturer certifications** — Certifications from brands like Moen, Delta, or Rinnai (for tankless) indicate trained installation and preserve warranties.
8. **Written workmanship warranty** — Minimum 1 year on labor; quality plumbers offer 2+ years.

Get at least three bids, and don't default to the lowest — mid-range quotes from insured, reviewed pros are usually the safest value.

**Q20: Questions to ask when hiring a local landscaping company for yard design**

1. **Credentials and Insurance** — "Are you licensed, bonded, and insured? Can I see proof of general liability and workers' comp?" Also ask about APLD (Association of Professional Landscape Designers) or state landscape contractor certifications.
2. **Portfolio and References** — "Can I see photos of completed projects similar in scope to mine, and contact three recent clients?" Visit completed projects in person if possible.
3. **Design Process and Timeline** — "Do you provide 2D/3D renderings? How many revisions are included? What's the timeline from design to installation?"
4. **Scope and Contract Details** — "What's included in the quote (plants, hardscape, irrigation, lighting, cleanup)? Are there line-item prices? What triggers change orders?"
5. **Plant and Material Warranties** — "What warranty do you offer on plants (typically 1 year) and hardscape (typically 2-5 years)? Who handles replacements?"
6. **Maintenance Planning** — "Will you provide a maintenance guide, and do you offer ongoing maintenance services? What are realistic water and upkeep requirements?"
7. **Site-Specific Expertise** — "How will you address my soil type, drainage, sun exposure, and local climate? Do you use native or drought-tolerant plants?"
8. **Permits and HOA Compliance** — "Who handles permits for hardscape, grading, or irrigation? Will you coordinate with my HOA?"
9. **Payment Schedule** — "What's your deposit requirement and payment milestone structure?" Avoid companies demanding more than 25-33% upfront.
10. **Subcontractors** — "Will your crews do the work, or do you subcontract? Who supervises the job?"

Get at least three written bids for comparison, and be wary of quotes significantly lower than competitors—it usually signals cut corners on materials or labor.
