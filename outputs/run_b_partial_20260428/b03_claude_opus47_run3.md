**Q18: Most important factors when selecting a local daycare for a toddler**

Visit at least three centers, unannounced if possible, and trust your instincts about how staff interact with children.

**Q20: Questions to ask a local landscaping company for yard design**

1. **"Are you licensed, insured, and bonded?"** — Request certificates of liability and workers' comp insurance; protects you from liability for on-site injuries or damage.
2. **"Do you have a landscape designer or architect on staff?"** — A degreed landscape architect (APLD or ASLA credentials) produces higher-quality designs than a crew foreman.
3. **"Can I see a portfolio of completed projects similar to mine?"** — Ask for 3-5 references of projects 2+ years old so you can see how designs mature.
4. **"What's your design process and what deliverables will I receive?"** — Look for site analysis, 2D plans, 3D renderings, and plant lists with sun/water requirements.
5. **"Do you self-perform or subcontract?"** — Understand who's actually on your property; subcontracted hardscaping is common but should be disclosed.
6. **"What warranty do you offer on plants and hardscaping?"** — Industry standard is 1 year on plants, 2-5 years on hardscaping installation.
7. **"How do you handle changes and overages?"** — Get a written change-order process; verbal agreements cause disputes.
8. **"Who handles permits and HOA approvals?"** — Clarify responsibility, especially for drainage, retaining walls, or significant grading.
9. **"What's included in maintenance vs. design-build?"** — Some companies only design; others offer ongoing care — decide what you need.
10. **"Can you provide a detailed line-item estimate?"** — Avoid lump-sum bids; itemization reveals where money goes and enables value engineering.

Also ask about sustainability practices, native plant use, and irrigation efficiency.
