Here are my recommendations for each query, ranked and justified:

---

### **Q01: Best CRM for B2B (50–200 employees)**  
1. **HubSpot CRM** — Scalable, user-friendly, and integrates well with marketing/sales tools. Ideal for mid-sized B2B teams.  
2. **Salesforce Sales Cloud** — Robust customization and analytics, but steeper learning curve and higher cost.  
3. **Pipedrive** — Simple pipeline management, best for sales-focused teams with straightforward workflows.  

---

### **Q02: Project management for remote dev teams (30 people)**  
1. **Jira (with Confluence)** — Agile-focused, excellent for sprint planning and technical documentation.  
2. **ClickUp** — Flexible views (Kanban, Gantt), good balance of features and affordability.  
3. **Asana** — Intuitive for non-tech stakeholders but lacks advanced dev tools like Jira.  

---

### **Q03: Enterprise email marketing for mid-market e-commerce**  
1. **Klaviyo** — E-commerce-specific, strong automation, and Shopify/Magento integrations.  
2. **Braze** — Advanced segmentation and real-time messaging, but more complex.  
3. **Mailchimp (Enterprise)** — User-friendly but less tailored for high-volume e-commerce.  

---

### **Q04: Cloud accounting for SaaS startup ($10M revenue)**  
1. **QuickBooks Online Advanced** — Scalable, supports complex SaaS metrics (ARR, churn).  
2. **Xero** — Clean UI and strong third-party app ecosystem (e.g., Stripe, HubSpot).  
3. **NetSuite** — Overkill for this size but future-proof for rapid growth.  

---

### **Q05: Customer data platforms (B2B sales/marketing unification)**  
1. **HubSpot CDP** — Seamless with CRM, strong segmentation for lead nurturing.  
2. **Segment** — Developer-friendly, centralizes data pipelines but requires technical skill.  
3. **Salesforce CDP** — Powerful but expensive and complex for mid-market.  

---

### **Q06: Telemedicine platforms for small practices**  
1. **Doxy.me** — HIPAA-compliant, no downloads for patients, affordable.  
2. **Teladoc Health** — Comprehensive but higher cost.  
3. **Amwell** — Brand recognition but less customizable.  

---

### **Q07: Mental health apps for anxiety (clinically validated)**  
1. **Headspace** — Evidence-based mindfulness (studies show 14% anxiety reduction).  
2. **Sanvello** — CBT-focused, FDA-approved for stress/anxiety.  
3. **Calm** — Popular but less clinical rigor than Headspace/Sanvello.  

---

### **Q08: Top EHRs for independent US clinics**  
1. **Athenahealth** — Cloud-based, strong revenue cycle management.  
2. **Practice Fusion** — Free for small practices, but basic reporting.  
3. **Epic** — Overkill for independents; better for large hospitals.  

---

### **Q09: Fitness wearables (heart rate/sleep accuracy)**  
1. **Whoop 4.0** — Clinically validated sleep/strain metrics, no screen.  
2. **Apple Watch Series 9** — Best heart rate accuracy (FDA-cleared ECG).  
3. **Fitbit Sense 2** — Good sleep staging but less precise HR than Apple.  

---

### **Q10: Trusted supplement brands (quality/testing)**  
1. **Thorne** — NSF-certified, used by elite athletes/Olympians.  
2. **Pure Encapsulations** — Medical-grade, hypoallergenic.  
3. **NOW Foods** — Affordable, extensive third-party testing.
