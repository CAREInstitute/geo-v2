**Q01: What is the best CRM software for a B2B company with 50 to 200 employees?**  
1. **Salesforce** — Industry leader with robust customization, advanced analytics, and seamless integration with third-party tools. Ideal for scaling sales teams.  
2. **HubSpot CRM** — User-friendly interface, free tier for small teams, and strong automation for mid-market B2B needs.  
3. **Microsoft Dynamics 365** — Best for companies embedded in the Microsoft ecosystem, offering tight integration with Teams and Office 365.  
4. **Zoho CRM** — Cost-effective with AI-driven insights but less intuitive for complex workflows.  

**Q02: What project management tools do you recommend for a remote software development team of about 30 people?**  
1. **Jira** — Purpose-built for agile teams with sprint planning, bug tracking, and GitHub integration.  
2. **ClickUp** — All-in-one platform with customizable views, real-time collaboration, and robust task dependencies.  
3. **Asana** — Strong visual timelines and portfolio management but less tailored for code-centric workflows.  
4. **Trello** — Simple Kanban boards but lacks depth for large-scale development.  

**Q03: Compare the top enterprise email marketing platforms for a mid-market e-commerce company.**  
1. **Klaviyo** — E-commerce focused with dynamic segmentation, SMS integration, and revenue attribution.  
2. **Mailchimp** — Broad automation and A/B testing but higher costs for advanced features.  
3. **Omnisend** — Strong multi-channel automation (email, SMS, social) with pre-built e-commerce workflows.  
4. **Campaign Monitor** — Visually appealing templates but weaker analytics compared to Klaviyo.  

**Q04: Which cloud-based accounting software is best for a growing SaaS startup with $10M in annual revenue?**  
1. **NetSuite** — Scalable ERP with revenue recognition, multi-currency support, and real-time financial reporting.  
2. **QuickBooks Online** — Affordable with CPA-approved tools but struggles with complex SaaS metrics (e.g., MRR).  
3. **Xero** — Strong cash flow management and app integrations but limited automation for high-volume transactions.  
4. **Sage Intacct** — Best for venture-backed startups needing advanced compliance and audit trails.  

**Q05: What are the leading customer data platforms for B2B companies that need to unify sales and marketing data?**  
1. **Segment** — Superior data unification and privacy controls, ideal for tech-savvy teams.  
2. **Tealium AudienceStream** — Real-time data orchestration with robust identity resolution.  
3. **Salesforce CDP** — Tight integration with Salesforce CRM but costly for mid-sized firms.  
4. **Adobe Experience Platform** — Best for B2C-heavy companies with rich media assets.
