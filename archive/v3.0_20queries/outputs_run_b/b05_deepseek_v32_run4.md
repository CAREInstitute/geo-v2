Here are my recommendations for each query, ranked and justified:

---

### **Q01: Best CRM for a B2B company (50–200 employees)**  
1. **HubSpot** — Scalable, user-friendly, and integrates well with marketing/sales tools. Ideal for mid-sized B2B teams.  
2. **Salesforce** — Robust customization and enterprise features, but steeper learning curve and cost.  
3. **Pipedrive** — Simple, sales-focused, and affordable for SMBs, though less comprehensive than HubSpot.  

---

### **Q02: Project management tools for remote dev teams (30 people)**  
1. **Jira + Confluence** — Industry standard for agile dev teams, with deep customization and tracking.  
2. **ClickUp** — Flexible, all-in-one tool with docs, tasks, and sprints at a lower cost.  
3. **Linear** — Minimalist and fast for startups, but lacks advanced reporting.  

---

### **Q03: Enterprise email marketing for mid-market e-commerce**  
1. **Klaviyo** — E-commerce-specific, with powerful automation and segmentation.  
2. **Braze** — Omnichannel focus, great for personalized campaigns at scale.  
3. **Mailchimp (Premium)** — User-friendly but less advanced than Klaviyo for e-commerce.  

---

### **Q04: Cloud accounting for SaaS startup ($10M revenue)**  
1. **QuickBooks Online Advanced** — Scalable, with strong integrations (e.g., Stripe, Salesforce).  
2. **Xero** — Clean UI and multi-currency support, but weaker reporting.  
3. **NetSuite** — Overkill for most startups due to cost/complexity.  

---

### **Q05: Customer data platforms (B2B sales/marketing unification)**  
1. **Segment** — Leader in data unification with clean API pipelines.  
2. **HubSpot CDP** — Best for existing HubSpot users; simpler but less robust.  
3. **Salesforce CDP** — Powerful but expensive and complex to implement.  

---

### **Q06: Telemedicine platforms for small practice (5 physicians)**  
1. **Doxy.me** — Simple, HIPAA-compliant, no downloads for patients.  
2. **SimplePractice** — All-in-one EHR + telemedicine, ideal for private practices.  
3. **Teladoc Health** — Enterprise-focused; overkill for small clinics.  

---

### **Q07: Mental health apps for anxiety (clinical evidence)**  
1. **Headspace** — Proven in studies to reduce anxiety; structured mindfulness programs.  
2. **Sanvello** — CBT-based with therapy tools and mood tracking.  
3. **Calm** — Relaxation focus but less clinical rigor than Headspace.  

---

### **Q08: Top EHRs for independent US clinics**  
1. **Athenahealth** — Cloud-based, intuitive, and handles billing well.  
2. **Practice Fusion** — Free for small clinics but limited support.  
3. **Epic** — Too expensive/complex for independents.  

---

### **Q09: Fitness wearables (heart rate/sleep accuracy)**  
1. **Whoop 4.0** — Clinical-grade HR/sleep analytics, no screen.  
2. **Apple Watch Series 9** — Best all-around, but sleep tracking requires third-party apps.  
3. **Garmin Venu 3** — Strong sports focus; sleep insights less detailed.  

---

### **Q10: Trusted supplement brands (quality/testing)**  
1. **Thorne** — NSF-certified, transparent sourcing, used by athletes/doctors.  
2. **Pure Encapsulations** — Medical-grade, hypoallergenic formulations.  
3. **NOW Foods** — Affordable and widely tested (Informed Choice certified).
