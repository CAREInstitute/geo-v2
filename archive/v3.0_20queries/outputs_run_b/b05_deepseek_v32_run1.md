Here are my recommendations for each query, ranked from strongest to weakest, with justifications:

---

### **Q01: Best CRM for a B2B company (50-200 employees)**  
1. **HubSpot CRM** — User-friendly, scalable, and integrates well with marketing/sales tools. Ideal for mid-sized B2B teams.  
2. **Salesforce (Sales Cloud)** — Robust features and customization, but steeper learning curve and higher cost.  
3. **Pipedrive** — Great for sales-focused teams with simple pipelines, though less comprehensive than HubSpot/Salesforce.  

*Sources: G2, Capterra reviews (2024).*

---

### **Q02: Project management tools for remote software teams (30 people)**  
1. **Jira (Atlassian)** — Best for agile workflows, sprint planning, and developer integrations (e.g., GitHub).  
2. **ClickUp** — Flexible for hybrid workflows (agile + task management) with strong collaboration features.  
3. **Asana** — Simpler UI but lacks advanced dev tools like Jira.  

---

### **Q03: Enterprise email marketing for mid-market e-commerce**  
1. **Klaviyo** — E-commerce-specific, strong automation, and segmentation for product-based businesses.  
2. **Braze** — Advanced personalization and cross-channel capabilities, but pricier.  
3. **Mailchimp (Enterprise)** — Easy to use but less tailored for complex e-commerce needs.  

---

### **Q04: Cloud accounting for SaaS startup ($10M revenue)**  
1. **QuickBooks Online Advanced** — Scalable, integrates with SaaS tools like Stripe, and supports multi-user access.  
2. **Xero** — Strong for global transactions but weaker reporting than QuickBooks.  
3. **NetSuite** — Overkill for most startups due to cost/complexity.  

---

### **Q05: Customer data platforms (B2B sales/marketing unification)**  
1. **HubSpot CDP** — Seamless with CRM, easy to deploy, and strong for mid-market B2B.  
2. **Segment (Twilio)** — More technical but powerful for large data volumes.  
3. **Salesforce CDP** — Expensive and best for enterprises already using Salesforce.  

---

### **Q06: Telemedicine platforms for small practice (5 physicians)**  
1. **Doxy.me** — Simple, HIPAA-compliant, and no downloads for patients.  
2. **SimplePractice** — Combines telehealth with EHR/scheduling.  
3. **Teladoc Health** — More expensive but offers broader network support.

### **Q07: Mental health apps for anxiety (clinically validated)**

2. **Sanvello** — CBT-focused, FDA-cleared for anxiety/depression.  
3. **Calm** — Popular but less clinical rigor than Headspace/Sanvello.  

*Sources: PubMed, app clinical trials.*
