**Q16: What to look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation**
1. **Licensed, insured plumber with renovation-specific experience** — This is the single most important criterion because kitchen renovations involve more than simple repairs: fixture placement, code compliance, drainage, venting, and coordination with cabinets and countertops all matter. Ask specifically for recent kitchen remodel examples, not just general plumbing work.
2. **Clear written scope and itemized estimate** — The best plumbers define exactly what is included: rough-in work, fixture installation, permits, disposal, shutoff replacements, line upgrades, and patching expectations. Itemization reduces surprise charges and makes it easier to compare bids accurately.
3. **Strong permit and inspection knowledge** — A good renovation plumber should know when permits are required and be willing to handle or clearly explain them. Anyone who casually suggests skipping permits on substantial kitchen work is a red flag.
4. **Good coordination with other trades** — Kitchen work succeeds when the plumber can coordinate timing with the GC, electrician, cabinet installer, and countertop team. Ask how they handle scheduling dependencies and what can delay their portion of the work.
5. **Local reputation, references, and warranty** — Look for recent reviews mentioning remodels, not just emergency service calls, and ask for a workmanship warranty in writing. A plumber who stands behind fixture installation and leak-related workmanship is much safer to hire.

**Questions to ask:** Have you done kitchens of this scope before? Who pulls permits? What happens if old pipes need replacement? What is excluded? How do change orders work?  
**Bottom line:** Choose the plumber who is most renovation-competent and clearest in writing—not necessarily the cheapest.
