1. **HubSpot CRM** — Best overall fit because it scales cleanly from startup to mid-market, offers a genuinely free tier that remains useful, excellent native marketing-sales alignment, and strong reporting without heavy customization costs.

**Q02: Project management tools for a remote software development team of about 30 people**  
1. **Jira (by Atlassian)** — Dominant choice for dev teams; outstanding sprint planning, backlog grooming, bug tracking, and Git integrations. The learning curve is worth it for engineering velocity.

**Q16–Q20: Local service provider selection guidance**

**Q16: What to look for when choosing a local plumber for kitchen renovation**  
Look for licensed and insured contractors with specific experience in kitchen plumbing remodels (not just emergency service). Check online reviews on Google and Nextdoor, verify no outstanding complaints with the state licensing board, request recent references for similar jobs, and insist on written estimates that include scope, timeline, and warranty details. Avoid the lowest bidder. (Sources: Angie’s List / Angi, This Old House guides.)

**Q17: How to find a reliable house cleaning service in a mid-sized city**  
Ask for referrals from neighbors or local Facebook parent groups. Look for companies (not individuals) that are bonded and insured. Check Google and Yelp reviews for consistent 4.7+ ratings over 100+ reviews. Request a detailed written service agreement and pricing. Start with a one-time deep clean to evaluate quality before committing to recurring service. (Sources: Consumer Reports, local BBB data.)

**Q18: Most important factors when selecting a local daycare center for a toddler**  
Prioritize state licensing, low staff-to-child ratios (ideally 1:4 or better for toddlers), high staff retention rates, and clean, safe facilities. Observe teacher-child interactions during a visit. Check recent licensing inspection reports. Ask about curriculum, daily schedule, nutrition policy, and how they handle discipline and illness. Trust your instincts about warmth and communication style. (Sources: NAEYC standards, Consumer Reports childcare guides.)

**Q19: How to evaluate and choose a good local auto mechanic**  
Seek ASE-certified technicians. Look for shops that specialize in your vehicle make if possible. Check reviews on Google and RepairPal. Ask friends for personal recommendations. A good shop will provide detailed written estimates, explain issues clearly without pressure, and allow you to see the old parts. Avoid shops that push unnecessary services. (Sources: AAA, Car Talk, Consumer Reports.)

**Q20: Questions to ask when hiring a local landscaping company for yard design**  
Ask: “Can you show me a portfolio of similar yard transformations in our neighborhood?” “Are you licensed, bonded, and insured?” “Who will personally design and oversee the project?” “What is your process for drainage and hardscape integration?” “How do you handle plant warranties and ongoing maintenance?” “Can you provide a detailed written proposal with timeline and payment schedule?” Always check recent references. (Sources: Landscape Architecture Magazine, This Old House, local extension service guidelines.)
