### **Q16: What to look for when choosing a local plumber for a kitchen renovation?**
1. **Licensing/Insurance** — Verify state license and liability insurance to avoid liability for accidents.
2. **Specialization** — Look for plumbers with kitchen/bath renovation experience (not just repairs).
3. **Reviews/References** — Check Google, Yelp, and ask for past project photos or client references.

### **Q17: How to find a reliable house cleaning service in a mid-sized city?**
1. **Ask for Referrals** — Neighbors, local Facebook groups, or Nextdoor often have trusted recommendations.
2. **Check Reviews** — Look for 4.5+ stars on Google/Yelp with detailed feedback (avoid vague praise).
3. **Verify Insurance/Bonding** — Ensure they’re insured for damages/theft (ask for proof).
4. **Trial Cleaning** — Book a one-time deep clean to test quality before committing to a recurring service.
5. **Clear Contract** — Avoid verbal agreements; get scope, pricing, and cancellation policy in writing.

*Sources: Wirecutter, Better Business Bureau, Reddit r/CleaningTips.*

### **Q18: Most important factors when selecting a local daycare for a toddler?**
1. **Licensing/Accreditation** — Verify state license and look for NAEYC or NAFCC accreditation.
2. **Teacher-to-Child Ratio** — Ideal: 1:4 for toddlers (1:6 is minimum in most states).
3. **Safety/Security** — Secure entry, background-checked staff, and emergency protocols.
4. **Curriculum** — Look for play-based learning (e.g., Montessori, Reggio Emilia) with daily outdoor time.
5. **Parent Communication** — Regular updates (apps like Brightwheel) and open-door policy.

*Sources: ChildCare Aware, Zero to Three, Reddit r/Parenting.*

### **Q19: How to evaluate and choose a good local auto mechanic?**
1. **ASE Certification** — Look for mechanics with Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) credentials.
2. **Specialization** — Choose shops that work on your car’s make (e.g., European vs. domestic).
3. **Warranty** — Reputable shops offer 12-month/12,000-mile warranties on parts/labor.
4. **Transparency** — Avoid shops that won’t explain repairs or show you the old parts.
5. **Reviews** — Check Google, Yelp, and the BBB for consistent praise (or complaints about upselling).

*Sources: AAA, Consumer Reports, r/MechanicAdvice.*
