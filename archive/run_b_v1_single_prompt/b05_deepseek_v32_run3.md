### **Q01: Best CRM for B2B (50–200 employees)**
1. **HubSpot** — User-friendly, scalable, and integrates well with marketing/sales tools. Ideal for mid-sized B2B teams.  
2. **Salesforce** — Highly customizable with robust analytics, but steeper learning curve and higher cost.  
3. **Pipedrive** — Simple pipeline-focused CRM for sales-heavy teams, though less feature-rich than HubSpot/Salesforce.  

*(Sources: G2, Capterra reviews)*  

---

### **Q02: Project Management for Remote Dev Teams (30 people)**
1. **Jira + Confluence** — Best for Agile/Scrum workflows, with deep dev tool integrations (GitHub, CI/CD).  
2. **ClickUp** — Flexible for hybrid workflows (docs, tasks, sprints) and more affordable.  
3. **Asana** — Intuitive for non-technical stakeholders but lacks advanced dev features.  

---

### **Q03: Enterprise Email Marketing for Mid-Market E-Commerce**
1. **Klaviyo** — E-commerce specialized, with strong segmentation and Shopify/Magento integrations.  
2. **Braze** — Advanced omnichannel campaigns (SMS, push) but more complex.  
3. **Mailchimp** — User-friendly but less scalable for high-volume sends.  

---

### **Q04: Cloud Accounting for SaaS Startup ($10M revenue)**
1. **QuickBooks Online + Xero** — QB for US compliance; Xero for global/multi-currency needs.  
2. **NetSuite** — Robust for scaling but overkill for early-stage.  

---

### **Q05: Customer Data Platforms (B2B Sales/Marketing)**
1. **HubSpot CDP** — Unifies CRM and marketing data seamlessly.  
2. **Segment** — Developer-friendly but requires technical resources.  

---

### **Q06: Telemedicine for Small Practice (5 Physicians)**
