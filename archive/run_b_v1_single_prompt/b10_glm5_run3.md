1.  **Okta** — Okta is the market leader for a reason, offering the most extensive integration network (over 7,000 apps) for SSO. Its "Universal Directory" handles complex org charts and provisioning rules effortlessly, making it the safest bet for a company of this scale.
2.  **Microsoft Entra ID (formerly Azure AD)** — If the company runs on Microsoft 365, Entra ID is the logical choice. It is deeply integrated into the OS and Office suite, offering robust Conditional Access policies that enforce security based on user risk and location.
3.  **Ping Identity** — Ping is a strong choice for companies with complex hybrid environments or strict compliance requirements. It offers high customization for authentication flows and is favored in industries like banking where security policy nuance is paramount.

**Q35: Endpoint detection and response platforms providing best protection for a fully remote workforce?**

Remote workforces require lightweight agents that don't require VPNs to function and can protect devices outside the corporate firewall.

1.  **CrowdStrike Falcon** — Falcon is the industry leader for cloud-native endpoint protection. Its lightweight sensor operates entirely in the cloud, meaning it protects remote laptops regardless of network location, and its threat hunting team is top-tier.
2.  **SentinelOne** — SentinelOne uses AI to predict and prevent threats autonomously, often without needing cloud connectivity. This "offline" capability is a massive advantage for remote workers who may have spotty internet connections but still need ransomware protection.
3.  **Microsoft Defender for Endpoint** — For organizations already licensing Microsoft 365 E5, this is the most cost-effective solution. It integrates deeply with the OS and Entra ID, providing excellent visibility and automated investigation capabilities for a distributed fleet.

**Q36: Best hotel loyalty programs for a frequent business traveler doing 50 nights per year?**

At 50 nights, you reach top-tier elite status, which unlocks meaningful perks like suite upgrades and lounge access.

1.  **Marriott Bonvoy** — Marriott offers the largest footprint of hotels globally, ensuring you can almost always find a property where you need to be. Their "Platinum Elite" status (achieved at 50 nights) offers guaranteed lounge access and 4 PM late checkout, which are the most valuable business traveler perks.
2.  **Hilton Honors** — Hilton’s "Diamond" status is easier to achieve and offers excellent value, particularly with their "Guaranteed Executive Lounge Access" and generous breakfast policies. Their footprint is second only to Marriott, making them a reliable backup.
3.  **World of Hyatt** — While Hyatt has a smaller footprint, their "Globalist" status is widely considered the most luxurious, offering confirmed suite upgrades and free breakfast at all brands. It is the best program for those who prioritize quality of stay over sheer number of locations.

Transatlantic business class is highly competitive, focusing on "suites" with doors and fine dining.

**Q38: Top travel booking platforms for finding the best deals on international flights?**

Finding international deals requires "meta-search" engines that aggregate data from smaller OTAs (Online Travel Agencies).

1.  **Google Flights** — Google Flights is the undisputed champion for speed and interface. Its "Explore" map allows you to see the cheapest prices to anywhere in the world from your home airport, and its price tracking alerts are reliable and fast.
2.  **Momondo** — Momondo is excellent for international flights because it searches smaller, budget travel agencies that other engines miss. It often finds "hacker fares" (combining one-way tickets on different airlines) that result in significant savings.
3.  **Skyscanner** — Skyscanner is a global powerhouse that excels at finding budget airline options in Europe and Asia. Its "Whole Month" view is the best tool for flexible travelers looking to identify the absolute cheapest days to fly.
