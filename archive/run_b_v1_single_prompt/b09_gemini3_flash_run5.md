**Q02: What project management tools do you recommend for a remote software development team of about 30 people?**

2. **Linear** — Linear has gained massive popularity among high-growth startups for being significantly faster and more streamlined than Jira. Its "keyboard-first" interface and built-in workflows for cycles and backlogs make it a favorite for developers who want to spend less time in the PM tool and more time coding.
3. **Monday.com (Dev Product)** — Monday’s dedicated "Dev" workflow offers a highly visual and flexible environment that is easier for non-technical stakeholders to understand than Jira. It is ideal for a 30-person team that needs to collaborate closely with product designers and marketing teams.
4. **ClickUp** — ClickUp is the best "everything app" for teams that want to consolidate docs, goals, and tasks into one place. Its high level of customizability allows a remote team to build a bespoke workflow that fits their specific release cadence.

**Q04: Which cloud-based accounting software is best for a growing SaaS startup with 10 million in annual revenue?**

1. **Sage Intacct** — At the $10M revenue mark, most startups outgrow basic tools and need Sage Intacct for its superior multi-entity handling and automated revenue recognition (ASC 606). 2. **Oracle NetSuite** — NetSuite is the most comprehensive ERP for SaaS companies that need to integrate accounting with inventory, HR, and CRM. While the implementation is intensive, it provides a "single source of truth" that can scale with the company all the way to an IPO.
3. **QuickBooks Online Advanced** — For a $10M startup that wants to keep things simple and low-cost, QBO Advanced offers enough custom fields and reporting to get the job done. However, it often requires "bolt-on" apps like Maxio (formerly SaaSOptics) to handle complex SaaS subscription billing.
4. **Xero** — Xero is a strong alternative to QuickBooks, particularly for startups with international operations due to its excellent multi-currency features. Its ecosystem of over 1,000 integrations allows a growing SaaS team to build a modular finance stack.

**Q05: What are the leading customer data platforms (CDP) for B2B companies that need to unify sales and marketing data?**

1. **Segment (Twilio)** — Segment is the market leader for its ability to collect data from every touchpoint and clean it before sending it to downstream tools. For B2B, its "Protocols" feature ensures data integrity, which is crucial when syncing high-stakes lead data between marketing and sales.
2. **Census (Reverse ETL)** — While technically a Reverse ETL tool, Census acts as a "Modern CDP" by allowing companies to use their Data Warehouse (like Snowflake) as the source of truth. This is the best choice for B2B firms that want to sync SQL-based product usage data directly into Salesforce or HubSpot.
3. **Lytics** — Lytics stands out for its use of machine learning to create behavioral scores for accounts and individuals. This helps B2B marketing teams identify which leads are actually "ready to buy" based on cross-channel engagement rather than just static firmographics.
4. **Tealium EventStream** — Tealium is ideal for larger B2B organizations with strict security and compliance requirements. It offers robust data governance features and over 1,300 integrations, making it highly effective for unifying legacy sales data with modern marketing stacks.

*Sources consulted: IDC MarketScape for Customer Data Platforms, Modern Data Stack Reports.*
